## CHAPTER 6 : THE NEXT THIRTY YEARS IN HEALTH AND WELL-BEING

#### THE 2020S: COMBINING AI WITH BIOTECHNOLOGY
- Medicine is not yet an exact science. Much of medicine is built on messy approximations that are usually mostly right for most patients but probably aren't totally fitting for an individual.
	- Automotive engineering is an exact science
	- The progress of IT will turn medicine into an exact science (e.g. drug discover, disease surveillance, robotic surgery)
	- in the past, medicine made plodding, linear progress but with AI one can amass experience from billions of procedures instead of the thousands a human doctor can perform in a career.
- the most important class of problems at present is designing treatments for emerging viral threats.
	- This is kind of like finding which key will open a virus's chemical lock from a pile of keys that could fill a swimming pool
	- AI now lets scientists sort through that gigantic pile faster achieving higher success rate
	- The US regulatory process typically takes a decade to bring a new drug to the market at an avg cost of $1.3B to $2.6B. Only 13.8% of candidate drug makes it to FDA approval.
-  In the past few years, AI assisted breakthroughs had increased noticeably. Its examples are :
	- Immune boosting drug against virus,
	- antibiotic that kills some of the most dangerous drug-resistant bacteria (analyzed 100 million types of antibiotics in a matter of hrs)
	- COVID-19 vaccines were made available just in 63 days. Before the pandemic vaccines took 5 to 10 years to develop
		- In 2021, USC developed an innovative AI tool to speed adaptive development of vaccines to combat COVID-19 variants 
- One of the fundamental challenge in biology : predicting how protein fold
	- In 2018, Alphabet's DeepMind created a program called AlphaFold to compete against the leading protein-folding predictors
	- In 2021, DeepMind publicly released AlphaFold 2 which can now achieve nearly experiment level accuracy for almost any protein given
- At present AI drug discovery is a human-guided process. 
	- But in the future AI will be able to search more creatively, identifying probs that human clinicians hadn't noticed and propose complex therapies
- AI will scale up modeling larger systems in simulation from proteins to tissues to organs. Eventually enabling us to cure diseases whose complexity puts them out of the reach of today's medicine 
	- e.g. cancers learn to resist its treatments
	- e.g. neurodegenerative diseases as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's involve subtle complex processes that cause misfolded proteins to build up in the brain and inflict harm.
	- AI simulations will enable us to understand the root causes of diseases and treat patients effectively long before they become debiliated.
- AI is also causing a revolution in the trials we use to validate them.
	- Using AI to assess how a drug would work for tens of thousands of (simulated) patients for a (simulated) period of years in a matter of hours or days
	- Simulations will be able to find numerous such correlations, yielding highly specific risk-benefit profiles for each individual patient
	- We'll be able to make meaningful progress on digitizing the entire human body by the end of 2020s
- Regulators will need to anticipate these emerging approaches and be proactive to make sure that the incentives are balanced between appropriate caution and lifesaving innovation
- In the near future, wearable trackers will be able to suggest treatment before someone even knows they are sick
- In the late 2020s AI powered tools will reach superhuman performance levels at virtually all diagnostic tasks
	- Medical imaging is one of the first fields to see AI's remarkable performance bc finding pixel pattern is what AI does best

#### THE 2030S AND 2040S: DEVELOPING AND PERFECTING NANOTECHNOLOGY
- Moravec argued in 1988, no matter how much we fine tune our DNA based biology, our flesh and blood systems will be at a disadvantage relative to our purpose-engineered creations
	- "Humans will only be second class robots." (Peter Weibel)
- A combination of AI and nanotechnology revolution will enable us to redesign and rebuild our bodies and brains and the worlds with which we interact
- Human neurons fire 200 times per second at most but retail computer chips exceed 5B cycles per second
- The first reference to nanotechnology was made by the physicist Richard Feynman in his 1959 lecture "There's Plenty of Room at the Bottom"
- In order for nanotech to have an impact on large objects it needs to have a self-replicating system
- In mid 1980s engineer K. Eric Drexler founded the modern field of nanotech. He designed an abstract machine that used atoms and molecular fragments which would feature a computer able to direct the placement of atoms
- The worst case scenario of nanotech would be so called gray goo meaning self replicating nanobots that form an uncontrollable chain reaction(more to come in chap 8)
- In 2001, Richard Smalley and Eric Drexler had a public debate on whether precise manufacturing using molecular assemblers will ever be possible
	- They differed their approaches. Drexler argue that top-down methods are the ultimate goal of nanotech, allowing fabricator machines to build nanobots from scratch
- Diamondoids are tiny cages of carbon atoms arranged by the most basic types of diamond crystals. These may be able to form the building blocks of extremely light strong nanoengineered structures
- One of the great challenges of nanoscale manufacturing is generalizability
	- Its relatively easy to design an assembler that can make one kind of substance but its another to design one capable of making different kinds of substances
- Once we have advanced nanomanufacturing, the cost of manufacturing any physical object would be only pennies per pound!
	- According to Drexler, the total cost of molecular manufacturing process fall around $2 per kg no matter what's being made, whether diamonds or food
- Past: books were expensive bc of they were copied by hand(heavy labor cost) vs. NOW: buying e-books means you are paying for creative assembly of information(almost zero cost to create books)
	- In this case e-book's value is dependant entirely on information
- In 2023, the value of physical products comes from many sources:
	- raw materials, manufacturing labor, factory machine time, energy costs, transportation
- In the future innovation will dramatically reduce most of these costs
	- raw materials will be cheaper to extract w/ automation
	- robotics will replace labor
	- factory machine will be cheaper
	- energy costs will fall due to solar photovoltaics and energy storage
	- autonomous vehicles will drive transportation costs down
- As these component's value will become less expensive but the proportional value of information contained in the products will increase
	- Meaning the information content of most products are going to increase
	- just like some of platform services like Instagram or Google products will be provided free and rely on other resources like ads to create revenue
	- companies or govt in the future can also offer products as incentives for watching ads or doing volunteer services
- Culture and politics will play a large role in determining how rapidly the economy changes
	- Wealthy elites could not hoard the new abundance bc its pointless to hoard when goods are truly abundant

#### APPLYING NANOTECHNOLOGY TO HEALTH AND LONGEVITY
- First generation of life extension : The phase we are now in. Applying current class of pharma and nutritional knowledge to overcoming health challenges
- Second generation of life extension : In the 2020s we are starting the second gen. Merger of biotech with AI
- Third generation of life extension : Using nanotech to overcome the limitations of our biological organs
- Supercentenarian aging is not a continuation or worsening of the same kinds of statistical risks of late adulthood
	- Supercentenarian aging : aging of ppl who are over 110 years old
	- Their body starts breaking down meaning they will have spontaneous kidney failure or respiratory failure
- In human body, the damage comes from 2 factors:
	- cellular metabolism: using energy to stay alive
	- cellular reproduction: mechanisms for self-replication
	- metabolism creates wastes in and around cells through oxidation but the body is able to remove this waste and repair the damage when it is young. However, as we age most of our cell reproduce over and over and errors start piling up faster than the body can fix
- This is why we need the ability to repair damage from aging at the level of individual cells and local tissues to cure aging itself
	- The most promising method as of now is having nanorobots to carry out the repair
- Longevity escape velocity: if one can live long enough until anti-aging research starts adding at least one year to ones' life expectancy annually, that will buy enough time to cure any facets of aging
- The idea of life extension often meet resistance but Kurzweil knows that ppl generally do not want to die unless they are in enormous pain
- Nanobots not only will repair and augment degrading organs but eventually will be able to help in efficiently placing substances into the blood supply or remove them
	- This will allow it to preserve body's normal function but also optimize it to the human's liking. (e.g. give more focus and energy, speed up body's natural healing and repair)
	- Using these nanobots should prevent major disease from even arising
	- Nanotech can help edit DNA effectively in the future. (e.g. turn off malfunctioning DNA)
	- At first nanotech treatments will be initiated by humans but ultimately they will be carried out autonomously
	- Nanobots may even replace our blood supply
	- People will be able to change their cosmetic appearances as they do in online games
- The most important role of nanotech will be augmenting the brain
	- A deeper virtual neocortex will give us the ability to think more complex thoughts 
	- By 2053 about $1,000 of computing power will be enough to perform around 7M times as many calculation per second as the human brain
- In 2040s - 50s we will be able to rebuild our bodies and brains vastly beyond what our biology capable of
	- e.g. swim and breath under water like a fish, think million times faster, even have wings if we wanted them
	- We will not be dependent on the survival of any of our bodies in order for us to live
