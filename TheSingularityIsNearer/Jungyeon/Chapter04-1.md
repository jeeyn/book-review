## CHAPTER 4 : LIFE IS GETTING EXPONENTIALLY BETTER

#### THE PUBLIC CONSENSUS IS THE OPPOSITE
- Day by day the world is becoming a better place to live which means positive trends have been progressing for years, but we never hear about them because they’re not exciting.
	- The world is getting better and continual progress is being made in diverse areas of social impact.
- The law of accelerating returns (LOAR) is the fundamental driver of many individual trends we see, and the result will be a dramatic improvement in most aspects of life in the very near future.
	- The law of accelerating returns does not apply to all forms of innovation.
		- e.g. Transportation technology doesn't create feedback loops.
	- LOAR is so powerful because feedback loops keep the cost of innovation lower than the benefits, so progress continues.
	- Due to this, most aspects of life will be getting exponentially better in the coming decades.
- News coverage and social media algorithms creates a selection bias towards stories about looming crisis while relegating the kinds of headlines cited at the beginning of this chapter to the bottom of our newsfeeds.
- For most of human history, improvements in the quality of life were so small and fragile that they would hardly be noticeable even over a full lifetime.
- In the modern days, people continually monitor their information sources for developments that might imperil them. Also, they remember the past as being better than it actually was.
- Nostalgia is more than reminiscence; it is a coping mechanism to deal with the stress of the past by transforming it. If the pain of the past did not fade, we would be forever crippled by it.
- But humans are conditioned to expect entropy, the idea that the default state of the world is things falling apart and getting worse
both this intense incentivizes populist politicians to promise to restore the lost glories of the past, despite that past being dramatically worse than nearly.
- Violent incidents will be reported extensively, whereas reductions in crime are literally non-incidents, so they do not get widely covered.
- The vast majority of “news” or information that is novel and timely consists of bad things.
- Most of the good things happening in the world are very gradual processes of progress.
- The introduction of social media aggregated alarming news from the entire planet, whereas previous generations were mainly focused on local or regional events.
- Daniel Kahneman, Nobel Prize winner, explains unconscious heuristics people make:
	- People systematically disregard prior probability.
	- They have a misunderstanding of regression to the mean.
	- People estimate the likelihood of an event by how easily they can think of examples of it.
- The big picture trends should remind us that, as hopeless as some problems may seem, as a species, we are turning the tide in solving them.

#### THE REALITY IS THAT NEARLY EVERY ASPECT OF LIFE IS GETTING PROGRESSIVELY BETTER AS A RESULT OF EXPONENTIALLY IMPROVING TECHNOLOGY
- Domestic appliances facilitated the liberating and transformative shifts that brought women into the workforce.
- Technological innovation promotes conditions that help more people in a society fulfill their potential, which in turn enables even more innovation.

#### LITERACY AND EDUCATION
- The introduction of the printing press in Europe in the late Middle Ages made it practical for ordinary people to become literate.
- By 1970, majority of the world's population had become literate, and computers were facilitating education and increasing the benefits of schooling.

#### AVAILABILITY OF FLUSH TOILET, ELECTRICITY, RADIO, TELEVISION, AND COMPUTERS
- Historically, one of the great causes of disease and death was contamination of food and water supplies by human feces.
	- Worldwide access to safe toilets has been steadily rising as sanitation technology becomes less expensive.
- Electricity is the prerequisite for the countless other benefits of modern civilization. For those still without power, the primary obstacle is political, not technological.
- The first transformative communication technology enabled by electricity was radio. Then came television.
- Unlike radio and television, which allowed passive media consumption, computers open up wider possibilities because they are interactive.
- PCs were entering American homes during the 1970s, and after Apple's famous 1984 Super Bowl commercial, the proportion of U.S. households with a computer nearly doubled within five years of its airing. The blossoming of the internet in the 1990s massively expanded the usefulness of computers.
- As the volume of worldwide internet traffic grew, the internet became more worthwhile to more people, creating a positive feedback loop.
	- Many people contributed content of their own, which increased the internet’s value even further.
- Computers embedded in smartphones have rapidly expanded market penetration in the developing world. As of 2022, two-thirds of the world's population has at least one.

#### LIFE EXPECTANCY
- Over time, humans developed ways to avoid or kill external pathogens, bacteria and viruses that bring disease from outside our bodies. Today, the uncurable diseases remaining spring mostly from deep within our own bodies, such as cancer, diabetes, Alzheimer's.
- First bridge to radical life extension: reducing the risk of disease through lifecycle diet and supplementation.
- The second bridge, combining AI and biotechnology to defeat degenerative diseases.
- In the 2030s, we will reach the third bridge of radical life extension: medical nanorobots with the ability to intelligently conduct cellular-level maintenance and repair throughout our bodies.
- By the fourth bridge, we will be able to back up our mind, files digitally, in 2040s.
- This would mean that even if the biological brain was destroyed, it wouldn’t extinguish the person’s identity. We would achieve an almost arbitrarily long lifespan by being copied and recopied as backups.

#### DECLINE IN POVERTY AND INCREASE IN INCOME
- With the spread of industrialization, poverty rates soon began to decline in Europe and America.
- In 2000, the UN announced the Millennium Development Goals, which coordinated international efforts to achieve key developments by 2015. Although many of the targets were not met, it stimulated very important progress that improved hundreds of millions of lives.
- Today’s poor are much better off in absolute terms due to the wide accessibility of free information and services via the internet.
- Personal income in the U.S. has risen enormously.
- Globalization has made available a much wider selection of products and services.
	- The maturation of 3D printing and nanotechnology will exponentially accelerate the diversification of our choices in the coming decades.
- Average income per hour worked in the U.S. has been steadily rising.
- Average hours worked in the U.S. per year have been declining and are at about Depression-era levels.
- Another indicator of socioeconomic well-being is child labor. Fortunately, child labor has been in steady decline throughout the twenty-first century.

#### DECLINE IN VIOLENCE
- Increasing material prosperity has a mutually reinforcing relationship with declining violence.
	- People with a lot to lose have stronger incentives to avoid fighting. And when people look toward long lives of safety, they have good reason to make investments that benefit society.
- Another factor that is coming to be properly appreciated: the relationship between pollution and crime.
	- Exposure to lead in car exhaust and household paints adversely affected children’s cognitive development, causing lowered impulse control.
- We are experiencing an expanding circle of empathy, as philosopher Peter Singer puts it.
	- As people become more confident that they will be free from violence, the incentives to build schools and to write and read books increase.
	- This encourages the use of reason instead of force to solve problems, which then reduces violence.
	- Expanding empathy has extended from narrow groups like clans to nations, then to people in other countries, and even to animals.
- As wealth grows and poverty declines, there are fewer zero-sum struggles over limited resources.
	- The digital revolution has already rolled back scarcity conditions for many things that can be easily represented digitally.

#### GROWTH OF RENEWABLE ENERGY
- The cost of environmentally friendly renewable energy has been dropping exponentially, and the overall amount of energy obtained from renewable resources is growing exponentially.
