## CHAPTER 4 :

#### SPREAD OF DEMOCRACY
- Cheap renewable energy will be abundant but sharing it will require democracy
	- Information technology makes society more democratic
- Theoretically, information is an abstract orderliness that contrasts with chaotic entropy.
- The physical mediums are important as it affects how well society can practically gather, store, manipulate, and transmit information.
- The more ideas a person/society has it becomes easier to share ideas and then create more new ones
- How technology made society more democratic:
	- The invention of Gutenberg's printing press in 1440 led to spread of news and ideas
		- Books became vastly cheaper which meant idea sharing became cheaper. 
		- Middle-class being able to afford books unlocked huge amount of human potential.
		- Spread of knowledge brought wealth and political empowerment. The idea that eligibility to vote did not depend on one's status at birth set the stage for universal political participation
	- In 1900s only 3% of world's population lived under what we would currently consider democracies. After WW1 it rose to 19%
		- The rising tide of fascism put democracy into retreat.
	- The radio enabled mass communication which initially helped fascists to take power but also helped the spread of democracy
		- The post war period saw a rapid spike in people living under democracy. During the Cold War reach of democracy stayed steady with 1 in 3 ppl living under democracy.
	- The proliferation of communication technology(Color TVs, LPs) made people living under govt that suppressed discontent. The percentage of ppl living under democracy rose to 56% after the break up of Soviet Union in 1999.
	- The world's population living under closed autocracies decreased to 26% in 2022 due to the use of social media. 
- A crucial challenge in the near future would be helping countries that fall into gray area to make transition to fully democratic governance. Kurzweil thinks careful use of AI will promote openness and transparency while minimizing abuse and spread of misinformation.


#### WE ARE NOW ENTERING THE STEEP PART OF THE EXPONENTIAL
- IT will make more progress in the next 20 years than it did in the past 200 years
	- LOAR will take IT into the steep parts of the exponential curve
- Price-Performance of Computation : how many computation per second can be performed for one dollar
	- 1939 : 0.0000065 comptation/sec per 2023 dollars
	- 1965 : 1.8 computations/sec per 1 dollar
	- 2024 : 130B computations/sec per 1 dollar
- Digital technology has many remarkable qualities but it does not show evidence of economic growth
	- One cannot eat/wear/live in IT. Meaning IT itself does not hold economic value like goods.
	- But, this will change. Goods like food and clothing are being made more economical by IT but those will actually become IT. Thus such goods will be subject to the same high deflation rates that we see for other ITs(meaning it will become cheaper to produce)
		- In late 2020s, we will be able to print out goods like clothing from a 3D printer for pennies
		- In 2030s objects can be created with atomic precision
		- Meat grown from cell cultures will displace factory farming
- Many products wont need a physical form bc simulated versions with realistic details will perform well in metaverse
- As brain-computer becomes more advanced full immersion VR will feed stimulated sensory data directly into our brains
- Cultured meat and vertical farming will enable free up more land
	- In the past, cities came about to make people live close together but this will also change
	- With the use of autonomous vehicles and more free land, humans will live wherever we want as longer commutes will be more practical. Also will be able to work and play in virtual realities


#### RENEWABLE ENERGY IS APPROACHING COMPLETE REPLACEMENT OF FOSSIL FUELS
- In the future nanotech will increase cell efficiency by enabling photovoltaic cells to capture energy from electomagnetic spectrum. 
	- Photovoltaics (PV) : convert sunlight directly into electricity using semiconducting materials (usually silicon) through the photovoltaic effect
- Technology will allow photovoltaics of the future to be thinner, lighter and installable in more surfaces.
	- Unlike solar panels used today, cells built with nanotech can take many forms: rolls, film, coatings, etc.
- Energy will continue to reduce in cost as AI will will applied to material discovery and device design
	- Renewables theoretically achieve complete coverage of world electricity by 2041
- Solar energy is more than enough to meet humanity's needs. Its just that current tech can only capture fraction(20%) of theoretical maximum(max is around 86%)
	- One of the main challenges of solar energy is energy storage tech. Store when it is generated to use it later.
Solar energy will dominate during 2030s


#### WE ARE APPROACHING CLEAN WATER ACCESS FOR EVERYONE
- Key challenge of the future: ensuring clean water to the earth's growing population
- Much of the world still lacks infrastructure to collect, kepp, and deliver clean water bc it is expensive.
- Nanoengineered materials will enable filters to work more efficiently and become cheaper


#### VERTICAL AGRICULTURE WILL PROVIDE INEXPENSIVE, HIGH-QUALITY FOOD AND FREE UP THE LAND WE USE FOR HORIZONTAL AGRICULTURE
- In the past vast majority of the population needed to work in agriculture to survive
- As agricultural methods became more efficient, more food became available and more people could spend their time on other activities like trade, science, and philosophy.
- Land efficiency improvement has been exponential that today we need less than 30% of the land that we needed in 1961
- Vertical agriculture is emerging as a solution to grow more crops in a limited outdoor area. Typically grown hydroponically in nutrient-rich water. Now some are aeroponics which replaces water with mist.


- Advantages of Vertical Farming: 
	- Vertical farming will free up more land for humans 
		- Agriculture is estimated to take up around half of world's habitable land
		- Gotham Greens uses 95% less water and 97% less land than traditional dirt farm
	- Less pollution from loose soil, pesticides
	- cities can grow and source crops locally which means not transporting crops long distance (= less pollution as well)

- In the future converging innovations in photovoltaic electricity, material science, robotics, and AI will make vertical farming much less expensive that consumers may be able to get food almost for free.
	- This is process mirrors what took place in IT as a result of LOAR. Vertical agriculture represents turning food production in to an information technology


#### 3D PRINTING WILL REVOLUTIONIZE THE CREATION AND DISTRIBUTION OF PHYSICAL THINGS
- Manufacturing solid objects took two forms : 1) shaping material inside a mold, 2) removing material from a block or sheet
	- They are both expensive and have major disadvantages
- In 1980s 3D printing emerged. 3D printing creates parts by stacking flat layers and building them up into a 3D shape
	- Initially they produced in plastic but now they can create objects out of many materials like paper, ceramic, metal, etc.
	- This enables inexpensive and fast prototyping for engineers
	- It will also allow objects like clothes, shoes or tools to be precisely fit the person using them. Vital medical implants will be cheaper and effective
	- Manufacturing will be decentralized which will empower consumers and local communities
	- It will create less pollution since products do not need to travel long distance
- Drawbacks of 3D printing
	- The spread of 3D printing will require us to come up with new approaches to protect intellectual property
	- People can print weapons. It will require us to reevaluate our current regulations.

#### 3D PRINTING OF BUILDINGS
- 3D printing can be used for buildings and it will dramatically lower construction cost
	- China is already a hub of 3D printed buildings. (WinSun, HuaShang Tengda)
- Advantages of 3D printing of buildings
	- cuts labor costs making basic housing affordable
	- shortens construction times
	- reduces environmental impact
	- uses locally sourced materials


#### DILIGENT PEOPLE WILL ACHIEVE LONGEVITY ESCAPE VELOCITY BY AROUND 2030
- Biological life is suboptimal bc evolution is a collection of random processes optimized by natural selection. This will soon change.
- By applying intelligence to biology we will be able to explore full range of genetic possibilities
- We are seeing exponential progress in genome sequencing
- By the end of 2020s biological simulators will generate data in hours rather than the years that clinical trials typically require.
	- The transition from human trials to in silico trials might raise concerns over simulations making a mistake in its analysis but if it is used well it can cover more factors like demographics and diverse diseases which will then provide how a new treatment will affect a patient in granular detail
		- In silico trials are computational simulations that replicate clinical trials using virtual patient populations to evaluate the safety and efficacy of drugs or medical devices

##### 4 Bridges to Radical Life Extensions
- *Bridge 1 : Health, Fitness, and Conventional Medicine*
	- This is the bridge we are currently crossing.
	- The goal here is aggressive maintenance of your current health using existing knowledge.

- *Bridge 2: The Biotechnology Revolution*
	- Kurzweil believes we are now entering the early stages of this bridge.
	- This involves the intersection of biology and information technology, where we treat biology as programmable software.

The future bridges to radical life extensions we are going to face:

- *Bridge 3: Medical Nanobots*
	- Evolution was not interested in strengthening the immune system against threats that appear later in life such as cancer and neurodegenerative diseases bc in the past ppl reproduced at a younger age and died younger.
	- Medical nanobots will monitor the blood supply and increase or decrease various substances including hormones, nutrients, oxygen, carbon dioxide, and toxins thus augmenting or replacing functions of the organs.
	- By the end of 2030s we will largely be able to overcome diseases and the aging process
	- Longevity escape velocity: a tipping point where ppl can add more than a ywar to our remaining life expectancy for each calendar year that passes

- *Bridge 4: Brain-Computer Interfaces*
	- This will augment our biological neocortex and make it a hybrid of biological thinking with digital extension. Later the digital portion will expand and ultimately predominate.
	- People will be able to choose to delete their backed up mind files which could increase security risks such as vulnerability to cyberattacks
	- To Qs that these tech will only be available to the future wealthy:
		- Such tech start out by being expensive with limited fuction but by the time the tech is perfected they become affordable. Believe in the exponential price-performance improvement inherent in information technologies.
			- *e.g.* as in the past cell phones were expensive but now its cheaper and it even performs better

#### THE RISING TIDE
- Information technology facilitates its own advancement (= LOAR) 
	- The vast improvements we've seen over the past two centuries in many areas was powered by this force
	- AI will make many linearly advancing technology into exponential information technology from agriculture to medical to etc. This will make life itself exponentially better in the time ahead.
