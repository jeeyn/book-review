## CHAPTER 7 : PERIL

#### PROMISE AND PERIL
- Technological progress will improve billions of lives but it will also increase perils of our species
- AI would need to specifically designed to avert accidents and thwart misuse
- Our civilization will overcome these perils bc the stakes are so high
- Peril brings out the best of human ingenuity

#### NUCLEAR WEAPONS
- Considering the # of warheads the world has now, even if an all-out nuclear exchange happens, it wouldn't kill everyone in the world. But nuclear fallout such as radio active materials and fire will cause severe global cooling and mass starvation
- MAD approach has deterred countries to not go off on a nuclear war
	- Mutually assured destruction(MAD) : An approach based on game theory that if one uses nuclear weapons the opponent will be met with an overwhelming retaliatory response.
- The advances in AI will lead to more effective tools for detecting and countering threats

#### BIOTECHNOLOGY
- advances in genetic engineering could create a supervirus that would have both extreme lethality and high transmittability
- Some of the Asilomar guidelines are baked into legal regulations governing the biotech industry
	- The 1975 Asilomar Conference on Recombinant DNA was a landmark meeting where scientists established voluntary safety guidelines to manage the risks of emerging genetic engineering technology
- AI augmented drug discovery can already enable potential vaccines or therapies for a newly emerging viral outbreak to be identified in a matter of days or weeks

#### NANOTECHNOLOGY
- As most risks of biotech have to do with self replication, in order for it to create a global catastrophe, nanotechnology has to be able to self replicate
- If nanotech matures, it would become much cheaper to manufacture weapons, unlike nuclear arsenals of today
- The worse case scenario is the grey goo which is a self replicating machines that consume carbon based matter and turn it into more self replicating machines
- grey goo could replicate itself fast but cannot move/travel very quickly so it would take weeks to wipe out the globe
- Instead of adding security features to inherently dangerous systems we must only build nanobots that are naturally fail safe
- we will need nanotechnology immune system before these catastrophes become a possibility
	- the main defense against grey goo would be blue goo which are nanobots that would neutralize their grey goo counterparts
	- the good nanobots should be deployed before the bad ones to ensure safety

#### ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE
- 3 categories of peril from superintelligent AI
	1) Misuse 
	- AI operators deliberately using AI to cause harm
	2) Outer Misalignment : 
	- Mismatch between the programmer's actual intentions and the goals they teach the AI in order to achieve them
	- Its difficult to specify exactly what you want when AI takes command literally
	3) Inner Misalignment : AI learns to achieve its goal produce undesirable behavior
- There is a field of tech research that is actively seeking ways to prevent AI misalignment
	- AI safety via debate : uses competing AI to point out flaws in each other's ideas
- Influential figures singed Lethal Autonomous Weapons Pledge but top military powers AKA US, Russia etc. have rejected it
- What will human even ultimately mean in the context of human control when we introduce a nonbiological addition to our own decision making in 2030s using brain-computer interfaces?
- Eliciting latent knowledge : trying to develop techniques that can ensure that if we can ask AI a question it gives us all the relevent info rather than just telling us what it thinks we want to hear
- Each step towards superintelligence is subject to market acceptance
- best way to avoid destructive conflict in the future is to continue the advance of our ethical ideals
- In 2030s we will have radical life expansion in addition to radical life extension
- AI can be used for both promise and peril
